About KAZOOMERANG

A small-town band with a hip, new sound.

Inspired by the Black-Eyed Keys, Alanis Moricassette, and the many pairs of sunglasses of Gwen Stefani.

We started our group one sumer night while watching an old re-run of Bonanza. Little Joe was in some big trouble with a gang of bandits. Scared, we hid beneath our hand-knitted blankets and waited as the most suspenseful moment of television history unfolded. At the worst possible time, the power went out. Driven mad from being deprived of this critical cinematic moment, we decided to start this band.

Our Band Members

Veronika Alves

Rag-timey vocals and Glass Armonica

After finding out she wasn't the most talented vocalist, Veronika searched for a new instrument in an attempt to launch a musical career. That was when she stumbled upon the other members of KAZOOMERANG, who introduced her to the glass armonica. Of course, Veronika still tries to throw her vocals into the mix whenever possible, which created a rag-timey style.

Kristopher Bullins

Turntables, Electric Jandals

After leaving his one man band "KazooHero" to go solo Kris became a very successful artist. He became known for his showmanship and pageantry within the music community of Seattle. He has the ability to involve the audiences in his performances created fans out of everyone who happened to attend children's parties. When word reached David Miller he sought out this rising star as a much needed addition to his newest project "KAZOOMERANG".

Peter Bellagh

Bass Kazoo, Backing Vocals

The newest member of the band, Peter Bellagh, started out by playing the saxophone at age 6. After completely mastering the sax at age 8, he wanted to switch to a more challenging instrument, so naturally, he picked up the Kazoo. During the next 10 years of his life, Peter struggled with internet addiction, bouncing from one rehab to another. He lived on the streets and made enough money for food through street performances, which is where the other members of KAZOOMERANG found him.

David Miller

Boomerang Xylophone, Bass Drum

Founding member David Miller began playing drums at age 13. While on a musical pilrimage to the Australian Outback, he invented his signature instrument, the Boomerang Xylophone. Thin steel wire suspends boomerangs of different lengths which emit various frequencies when struck.
